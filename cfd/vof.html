VOF Method for Combustion Simulation

Home CFD & Radiation Tech Reviews

The Volume-of-Fluid (VOF) method is a numerical technique for tracking and locating the free surface or fluid–fluid interface within computational domains. It is widely used in multiphase flow problems, including spray combustion simulations, to capture liquid–gas interactions.

Advantages: Conserves mass of all phases, handles large interface deformations. Limitations: Smeared interface representation, not ideal for sub-grid-scale interfacial dynamics.
