Monte Carlo Method in Radiation

Home CFD & Radiation Tech Reviews

The Monte Carlo method is a statistical approach for solving the Radiation Transport Equation (RTE). It involves tracing a large number of photon or particle paths through a medium, accounting for absorption, scattering, and emission events. While computationally expensive, it provides highly accurate solutions, making it a benchmark for validating other models.

Applications: Solar energy systems, combustion chambers, atmospheric radiation studies.
